About

An engineering student who ships, leads, and writes.

Equally at home soldering a microcontroller, anchoring a national-level event, editing a department publication, or building a community tool from scratch.

I'm Mohamed Afnnan Veettilayil, a first-year B.Tech Electronics and Instrumentation Engineering student at the Federal Institute of Science and Technology (FISAT), Angamaly, Kerala. My focus sits at the intersection of embedded systems, instrumentation, and real-world community impact.

Outside of coursework, I serve as a Google Student Ambassador, an ISA Student Representative, the IDEA Lab Student Representative at FISAT, and the sole Chief Editor of Metron, the EIE department newsletter that reaches over 240 students and 20 faculty members.

I build things that get used: an EVM still running school elections, an SGPA calculator used by 40+ students, an open-source ATS resume guide adopted in workshops, and an admissions helpdesk that walked 50+ first-generation applicants through KEAM counselling. I care more about what reaches people than about what stays on a slide.
